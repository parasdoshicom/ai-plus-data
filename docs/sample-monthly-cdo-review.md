# Sample Monthly CDO Review

This is a synthetic example. It uses fake but realistic metrics to show how a CDO or head of data could report AI adoption without exposing private company data.

## Executive Read

| Area | Status | Current read | Decision needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Business impact | Green | Recurring analytics work is getting faster in the first two pilot domains | Approve one additional domain after trust review |
| Trust and quality | Yellow | Answer consistency improved, but caveat handling is still uneven | Keep expansion gated on metric packet completion |
| Team leverage | Green | Managers report less first-pass analysis time on recurring questions | Standardize the workflow in manager scorecards |
| Governance | Yellow | Trusted-source coverage is improving but not yet broad enough | Deprecate duplicate dashboards before adding new AI surfaces |

## KPI Snapshot

| KPI | Current month | Prior month | Target | Read |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Recurring questions answered with approved source path | 68% | 44% | 75% | Improving, still below expansion bar |
| Top-10 metric packets verified | 7 / 10 | 3 / 10 | 10 / 10 | On track |
| Answer consistency across repeated phrasings | 86% | 71% | 90% | Needs final push |
| Reconciliation loops on pilot metrics | 6 | 14 | <5 | Meaningfully down |
| Manager-reviewed AI outputs | 42 | 18 | 40 | Adoption is real in pilot teams |
| High-risk outputs escalated instead of answered | 9 | 4 | Monitor | Good sign if escalations are appropriate |

## What Changed

1. The first pilot domain moved from ad hoc prompting to approved answer paths for most recurring KPI questions.
2. Managers started reviewing workflow quality, not just final answers.
3. Duplicate dashboards were identified as the main source of recurring trust friction.
4. The team found that caveat language matters as much as SQL correctness for executive use.

## Risks

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Premature expansion | The organization may mistake pilot success for broad readiness | Require 10 verified metric packets before adding another domain |
| Dashboard conflict | AI can amplify conflicting sources faster than humans can reconcile them | Name one blessed artifact per pilot metric |
| Manager inconsistency | Different review standards create uneven trust | Add the manager scorecard to the monthly review |
| Caveat loss | Correct numbers can still create bad decisions if caveats disappear | Require caveat checks in every trusted answer path |

## Decisions Requested

1. Approve dashboard deprecation for duplicate pilot-domain dashboards.
2. Keep AI analytics expansion gated until all 10 metric packets are verified.
3. Select the next candidate domain, but do not launch until the current pilot reaches the trust threshold.

## Next 30 Days

- Verify the remaining 3 metric packets.
- Add 15 more eval questions for ambiguous and caveat-heavy scenarios.
- Deprecate or relabel duplicate dashboards in the pilot domain.
- Run one manager calibration session on answer review quality.
- Produce one decision memo from the next weekly operating review.

## Board-Level Takeaway

The pilot is creating leverage because it is narrowing the problem before scaling it. The next leadership decision is whether to protect that discipline or let broad adoption outrun trust.

# Case Study: Consolidating Fragmented Analytics Teams

## Situation

Multiple analytics groups exist across the organization, each doing useful work, but the overall system is fragmented.

Symptoms:

- duplicate work across teams
- different standards for similar questions
- leadership sees multiple entry points into analytics
- headcount exists, but leverage stays low

## Intervention

The operating change is not just an org chart change. It includes:

1. consolidating overlapping functions around shared priorities
2. defining where central standards matter most
3. clarifying cross-functional ownership boundaries
4. standardizing review expectations and trusted artifacts
5. building stronger manager and leadership layers inside the unified org

## Before And After

| Dimension | Before | After |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Team structure | overlapping analytics groups | one more coherent operating layer |
| Priority setting | fragmented | clearer and more comparable across domains |
| Output quality | inconsistent by team | stronger shared standards |
| Leadership leverage | diluted | higher because the system is easier to steer |

## Signals Of Change

The data organization behaves more like one system:

- less duplication
- clearer priorities
- stronger consistency in output quality
- higher leverage for leaders and domain teams

## Transferable Lesson

Org design matters because AI and self-serve work best when the trusted surfaces, standards, and ownership model are legible. Consolidation is valuable when it increases coherence, not just control.

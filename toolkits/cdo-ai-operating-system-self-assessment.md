# CDO AI Operating System Self-Assessment

Use this when you want a fast read on whether your data organization has an AI operating model or just scattered experiments.

Score each area from 1 to 5.

## 1. Decision-Loop Alignment

1 = AI work is detached from the business's main decision forums
3 = a few recurring workflows are tied to real decisions
5 = AI adoption is clearly connected to the company's main operating cadence

## 2. Trust Layer

1 = critical metrics have multiple definitions and fuzzy ownership
3 = some important metrics are governed and trusted
5 = core metrics have one definition, clear ownership, and trusted retrieval paths

## 3. Workflow Standardization

1 = each analyst or manager improvises their own AI workflow
3 = a few reusable patterns exist
5 = the team has clear default workflows for framing, validating, and sharing AI-assisted work

## 4. Governance And Safety

1 = safe data boundaries and review rules are unclear
3 = some boundaries exist but enforcement is inconsistent
5 = data access, human review, and deprecation rules are explicit and followed

## 5. Manager Operating Capability

1 = managers mainly ask whether the team is using AI
3 = managers review prompts and tools, but unevenly
5 = managers coach workflow quality, use-case selection, and review discipline

## 6. Tooling And Context Quality

1 = AI works on raw warehouse sprawl and fragmented dashboards
3 = a few curated assets exist
5 = trusted metrics, examples, and preferred artifacts are easy for humans and AI to find

## 7. Executive Cadence

1 = leadership only hears about AI through isolated demos
3 = progress is discussed occasionally with some structure
5 = leadership reviews AI adoption through a repeatable brief covering impact, trust, leverage, and next decisions

## 8. Business Impact Evidence

1 = there is energy, but little measurable outcome
3 = a few visible wins exist
5 = the team can show time saved, better coverage, stronger trust, or faster decisions in recurring workflows

## Total Score

- 8-16: experimentation without an operating system
- 17-24: promising pockets, but weak consistency
- 25-32: real operating model taking shape
- 33-40: strong AI-native data operating system

## How To Use The Result

### If your weakest area is Decision-Loop Alignment

Start with one recurring business forum and design around that workflow first.

### If your weakest area is Trust Layer

Narrow to a small set of critical metrics and make the official definition explicit.

### If your weakest area is Workflow Standardization

Create one reference workflow with a clarification step, validation step, and manager-ready output format.

### If your weakest area is Governance And Safety

Document safe data boundaries, review gates, and what must stay human-led.

### If your weakest area is Manager Operating Capability

Train managers to review workflow quality and trust signals, not just prompt cleverness.

### If your weakest area is Tooling And Context Quality

Curate the trusted dashboards, queries, and examples AI should prefer.

### If your weakest area is Executive Cadence

Use a one-page board brief so leadership can review progress without falling into a tooling demo.

### If your weakest area is Business Impact Evidence

Measure one recurring workflow end to end: baseline time, error classes, adoption, and decision speed.

## Ten-Minute Review Questions

- Which recurring workflow should become our reference example?
- Which metrics must be official before scale?
- Where is human review still mandatory?
- Which stale dashboards or assets should be deprecated first?
- What is one measurable business outcome we expect from the next 30 days?

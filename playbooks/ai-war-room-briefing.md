# AI War Room Briefing

When a team asks, "What changed this week and what matters?" AI can help produce a sharp operating briefing, not just a pile of charts.

This playbook generalizes a pattern for fast, repeatable weekly or daily performance briefings.

## Goal

Produce a concise briefing that answers:

- what moved
- why it moved
- whether the move is signal or noise
- where to focus next

## Inputs

A solid AI war room briefing usually combines:

- core metrics and trends
- funnel stage performance
- segment or market movers
- experiment or initiative context
- blocker or incident signals
- a benchmark or prior period comparison

## Recommended Structure

1. Headline numbers
   The core KPI, recent trend, and whether the move is material.
2. Decomposition
   Separate the move into volume, conversion, mix, pricing, or other major effects.
3. Funnel view
   Show which step changed the most.
4. Segment movers
   Identify the biggest contributors by region, product, channel, cohort, or customer segment.
5. Signals and blockers
   Pull in qualitative context from incidents, launches, changes, or active constraints.
6. What to do next
   End with one to three focused follow-ups.

## Operating Principles

- Use trusted metrics only.
- Compare against a stable benchmark, not just yesterday.
- Distinguish noise from durable movement.
- Keep the output short enough to read in a standup.
- Link every observation to a next action when possible.

## Good Questions for AI

- Which stage contributed most to the weekly decline?
- Which segments explain most of the delta?
- Is this move large relative to trailing variation?
- What changed operationally in the same period?
- What should the team investigate first?

## What to Avoid

- unvalidated metric definitions
- over-precision in low-volume segments
- pure chart dumps with no prioritization
- explaining every movement instead of the most decision-relevant ones

## Suggested Output Template

```text
Headline:
Weekly conversion fell 2.1 points week over week, driven mostly by lower checkout completion.

Main drivers:
- traffic mix shifted toward lower-intent channels
- mobile checkout completion weakened
- one recent product change likely increased form friction

Signal check:
This move is outside the recent normal range and appears durable, not noise.

Where to focus:
1. mobile checkout experience
2. low-intent paid traffic quality
3. first-step funnel friction introduced this week
```

The briefing should help a team act, not just observe.
